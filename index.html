About the Project

ACKNOWLEDGE : The Mahabharata, regarded as the treasure of one’s life, teaches us how to live life in the most ethical yet the most optimal way. However, in the present days, people tend to label our culture as "backward". We all need to ACKNOWLEDGE the role our culture plays in our life from a young age. We aim to instill these very values in children.

BELIEVE : Mahabharata is not just like any other tale. It speaks volumes about constructive living or so to say the 'Art of living', which is extremely important for the children today. We want to make children BELIEVE in the importance of the morals and ethics in life, whose foundation is our dharma and Hindu culture.

CONNECT : The Mahabharata enables children to understand the basic ethics that one should have in their life to lead it in an exemplary manner. We want to CONNECT with the children and CONNECT them to our culture.

Purpose

  • Children, as the nation-builders of tomorrow, need to learn about their roots and culture, only then can they grow up with the spirit of appreciating and preserving cultural diversity and unity.

  • Knowing our roots from a young age is important.

  • Good morals enable us to become good humans.

  • They help us develop our value and belief system.

  • Moral beliefs of faith, integrity, freedom, personal responsibility, strong work ethics, and values of selflessness are desirable traits as a global citizen of tomorrow.

Importance of ABC

  • Nowadays, we idealize Western culture and have begun isolating our own culture.

  • At a young age, children have a very impressionable and mouldable personality.

  • They absorb everything they hear and see. It is important for them to absorb these morals and values at this age because our culture offers a sense of belonging which binds us to our roots.
